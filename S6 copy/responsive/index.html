Five Easy Ways To Design Your Ideal Room

The best way to fulfill the room of your dreams is by planning it first. Perfect designs come to life with trial and error. Trying what looks best before you set gives you a better look on the amount of potential styles your room could have. This is why designers work with a huge amount of requirements before assembling their design. And that's also why they aren't cheap! We talked to many of our insider interior designers and came up with easy ways to help you bring to life the room of your dreams all by yourself.
Often starting with the help of mood boards, designers can narrow down the elements of their design. They can divide their mood boards by color palette, textures, furniture pieces, spaces, measurements, etc. Designing a room can be a fun and creative process, but it can also be time-consuming. For people who have no prior experience in design or who lack a creative eye, it can be downright daunting. To make the process easier, start by figuring out your personal style and what type of ambiance you want the room to convey. Then, find ideas for the particular space you want to design, and create a blueprint of what you want the final product to be. Finally, make your purchases and design your space, knowing it's all yours from start to finish!

Why You Should Read The Bohemians

Written by Justina Blackeney, the architectural and interior design based book has turned into a best seller. With it's amazing colors and textures waiting to inspire you to turn your home into a bohemian hut, Blackeney shares DIYs and the best furniture combinations. The book is filled with the history of furniture pieces and the origin of the bohemian style. It answers your questions such as, "Why is this style so popular right now?." The Bohemians makes the boho style look so complicated. However, it is quite effortless. It basically joins every furniture piece that you wouldn't think would go together. LA-based designer Justina Blakeney defines the New Bohemians as creative individuals who are boutique owners and bloggers, entrepreneurs and ex-pats, artists and urban farmers. They embrace free-spirited, no-rules lifestyles and apply that attitude to all areas of their existence, including their homes. With little distinction between work and play, the new boho home often includes an office, art gallery, showroom, photography studio, restaurant, or even a pop-up shop. The New Bohemians explores 20 homes located primarily on the East and West coasts. Exclusive interviews with the owners, as well as 12 DIY projects created by Blakeney and inspired by objects found in the homes, o ffer insight into achieving this aesthetic. In addition, each home is accompanied by an Adopt-an-Idea section that offers general decor, styling, and shopping tips for easy duplication in your own home.
